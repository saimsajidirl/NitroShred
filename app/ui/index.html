What is NitroShred?

NitroShred permanently deletes your private files so they can never be brought back — not with recovery software, not by a technician, not by anyone.

Why isn’t the Recycle Bin enough?

When you delete a file normally, your computer doesn’t actually erase it. It simply marks that space as “available” and hides the file from you. The actual content stays on your disk until something else overwrites it.

That’s why “undelete” and recovery tools exist — they can often restore deleted files in seconds. Emptying the Recycle Bin does not truly destroy the data.

What does NitroShred do?

  1. Overwrites your files Every byte is replaced with zeros, like scribbling over a document until nothing is readable.
  2. Removes the file completely The file name and trace are wiped so there’s nothing left to find.
  3. Uses the fastest safe method for your storage On modern SSDs, NitroShred can ask the drive to erase data directly at the hardware level when possible.

Folder or drive — what’s the difference?

Shred a folder

Use this when you want to permanently destroy everything inside one folder — old tax documents, photos, backups, or any private files in a specific location.

Wipe a drive

Use this when you’re clearing an entire USB stick, external hard drive, or secondary drive before selling, donating, or disposing of it. Every file on that drive will be destroyed.

When should I use it?

  • Before selling or giving away a computer or external drive
  • When getting rid of sensitive personal documents
  • When you need files gone for good — not just hidden

Important safety notes

  • This cannot be undone. Once shredded, your files are gone forever.
  • Your main system drive is protected. NitroShred will not wipe Windows or your primary C: drive, so you can’t accidentally destroy your computer.
  • Double-check before you click. Make sure you’ve selected the right folder or drive.
