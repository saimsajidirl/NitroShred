What is NitroShred?

NitroShred permanently deletes your private files so they can never be brought back — not with recovery software, not by a technician, and not by law enforcement forensic labs.

When you shred with NitroShred, the data is overwritten and destroyed at the source. Even the FBI cannot recover it from that drive or card afterward.

Why isn’t the Recycle Bin enough?

When you delete a file normally, your computer doesn’t actually erase it. It simply marks that space as “available” and hides the file from you. The actual content stays on your disk until something else overwrites it.

That’s why “undelete” and recovery tools exist — they can often restore deleted files in seconds. Emptying the Recycle Bin does not truly destroy the data.

What does NitroShred do?

  1. Overwrites your files Every byte is replaced with zeros, like scribbling over a document until nothing is readable.
  2. Removes the file completely The file name and trace are wiped so there’s nothing left to find.
  3. Wipes all free space (Drive mode) After deleting files, NitroShred fills every remaining free cluster with zeros so old deleted data can’t be recovered.
  4. Hardware TRIM on SSDs On modern SSDs, NitroShred sends TRIM commands so the drive firmware permanently erases blocks at the hardware level.
  5. Raw sector wipe (Physical mode) Bypasses the file system entirely and zeroes every single physical sector — the drive looks identical to one fresh out of the factory. No ghost, no trace, no proof anything was ever there.

Folder, Drive, or Physical — which do I pick?

Shred a folder

Permanently destroy everything inside one folder — old tax documents, photos, private backups. The rest of the drive is untouched.

Wipe a drive

Full secure wipe of an entire USB stick or external drive — shred all files, fill every free cluster, TRIM the drive. Use before selling or donating storage.

Physical disk wipe

The deepest level of erasure. Bypasses the file system and zeroes every single physical sector on the disk. Use when Drive mode is not enough, or when you need absolute certainty.

Drive vs Physical · The key distinction

The ghost vs. the blank page

Drive mode destroys what the data said, but its ghost remains. A forensic expert might still be able to tell that something once existed in that space — they just cannot read what it was. Like a redacted document: the black bars are there. You know something was written. You just cannot read it.

Physical mode erases the ghost too. Every sector is overwritten with zeros until the entire drive is indistinguishable from one that has never been touched. There is no trace, no record, no proof that anything was ever there. It is as if the data never existed at all — not hidden, not redacted, simply gone from existence.

For most people, Drive mode is more than enough. Physical mode is for when you need the drive to look like it just came out of the box — no history, no past, nothing.

When should I use it?

  • When getting rid of sensitive personal documents or private photos

  • When preparing a device for decommission or disposal
